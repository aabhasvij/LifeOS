3:33 – 6:02
Strength Training
▼

Muscle mass is the #1 predictor of longevity and metabolic health. Resistance training 3×/week protects bone density, insulin sensitivity, and prevents age-related decline. Compound movements (deadlifts, squats, presses) are prioritized over isolation work.

KEY TAKEAWAY
Train for the 80-year-old version of you. Strength is survival.
🚶
6:02 – 7:47
Zone 2 Cardio (Low Intensity)
▼

Zone 2 is 60–70% max HR — a pace where you can hold a conversation but it's slightly effortful. This trains mitochondrial density and fat oxidation. 150–180 min/week is the target. Brisk walking, light jogging, cycling all count.

KEY TAKEAWAY
This is your aerobic base. Most people skip it chasing intensity.
🔥
7:47 – 10:39
Zone 4–5 Cardio (High Intensity)
▼

Zone 4–5 is 85–100% max HR. Used in small doses (1–2×/week), it spikes VO2 max — one of the strongest longevity biomarkers. Protocol: 4 min all-out effort, 4 min recovery, 4–5 rounds. Your Sunday football match already covers this.

KEY TAKEAWAY
VO2 max improvement = years added to your life.
🧘
10:39 – 11:59
Mobility & Flexibility
▼

Often neglected, mobility is what lets you keep training as you age. Focus on hip flexors, thoracic spine, hamstrings, and ankles. 10–15 min daily is sufficient. Dynamic stretching before training, static after.

KEY TAKEAWAY
Flexibility is your insurance policy against injury.
⚖️
11:59 – 13:27
Balance
▼

Balance training is the most underrated pillar. Single-leg work, proprioception drills, and unstable surface training protect you from falls and build deep stabilizer muscles. Directly translates to football agility.

KEY TAKEAWAY
Balance declines fast if untrained. It compounds fast if trained.
⚽
Football = Your HIIT Session
The 30–40 min match hits Zone 4–5 naturally. No separate HIIT needed. Sunday football checks your high-intensity box for the week. Saturday is intentionally light — fresh legs going into Sunday means better performance on the pitch.
📊
Zone 2 Is Your Biggest Gap
At 105 kg, fat oxidation and aerobic base need the most work. Tuesday and Saturday Zone 2 sessions plus Thursday brisk walk are non-negotiable. This is where body composition changes happen.
💪
Progressive Overload — The Only Rule
Add weight or reps each week on big lifts (Squat, Deadlift, Press, Row). Even 2.5 kg added per week on 3 lifts means you're 30+ kg stronger in 10 weeks. Track it.
🧘
Mobility After Football Is Critical
At your size, hip flexors and hamstrings take a beating in football. 10 minutes of static stretching post-match dramatically reduces injury risk and next-day soreness. Don't skip it.
⚖️
Balance Work = Football Performance
Single-leg work (Bulgarian split squat, single-leg RDL, BOSU) isn't just for longevity — it directly improves your agility, cutting ability, and injury resilience on the pitch.
🔄
Reassess in 6 Weeks
After 6 weeks your fitness baseline will have meaningfully shifted. Add a 5th strength session, extend Zone 2 duration, or increase intensity on high-intensity days.
