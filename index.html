Pre-conversation checklist
  • Take 2–3 recruiter calls — get real market number
  • Write 2 concrete project contributions with business impact
  • Rehearse saying "40 LPA" out loud until voice doesn't waver
  • Request 1:1 — frame as "growth direction" conversation
  • Choose a low-stress day — not Monday morning, not before deadline
Conversation playbook
0
Mindset before you walk in
Hold these three things
▾
You are not asking for a favour. You are sharing market information and inviting alignment. 40 LPA is a correction, not an ambition. She needs this conversation as much as you do.
1
Open — set the tone
30 seconds. Signal intent.
▾
"I wanted to have a conversation I've been meaning to have — about my growth here, where I see myself going, and making sure we're aligned on that. Is this a good time to go a bit deeper?"
Wait for her to settle in. The pause after this line is intentional.
2
Contribution anchor
Show the weight, not the list.
▾
"In the past year I've owned my projects end to end — from problem framing through to delivery at the client's end. What isn't always visible is the complexity underneath — the modelling decisions, the pipeline work, the iterations before anything reaches the client. I've been operating independently on that consistently."
Pick 1–2 specific projects mentally before the meeting. Don't volunteer a list unprompted.
3
Market reality
Factual. Calm. Not a threat.
▾
"I've been doing some honest homework on where my profile sits in the market — the combination of data science and credit risk domain depth. There's a meaningful gap between my current compensation and what the market is offering. I'm not saying that to create pressure — both of us should be working with the same information."
Pause after this. Let it land. Don't fill the silence.
4
The ask
Specific. No range. No softening.
▾
"What I'd like to understand is — what does a path to somewhere around 40 LPA look like here, and over what kind of timeline? I want to plan around that, and I want to make sure my work here continues to justify it."
Say 40. Not "35 to 40." 40 is the anchor.
5
What's in it for her
Make her your champion.
▾
"I'm aware you're building your team in a new capacity now, and I'd genuinely like to be someone you can count on as part of that. I think there's a lot we can do together if we get the foundations right. That's part of why I'd rather have this conversation now than let it simmer."
She's a new AVP. Retention is her problem. Your growth is her narrative upward.
6
Alliance close
Invite honesty. Settle intent.
▾
"I'd genuinely like to build my career here rather than keep switching. But I also need to feel like the growth is real and there's a clear path forward. I trust you to be straight with me about what's possible."
↓
Decision tree — her responses
Four branches. Know them before you walk in.
▾
Branch 1 — Engages warmly
"I hear you, let me see what I can do."
"Can we set a timeline? Even a 6-week checkpoint would help." — Convert goodwill into commitment.
Branch 2 — Defers to process
"Compensation goes through the cycle."
"I want to align on the goal now — so when the cycle comes we're already on the same page."
Branch 3 — Pushes back
"40 is quite a jump."
"I expected that. I'm not anchoring at 40 arbitrarily. What's your read on it?" — Hold the number.
Branch 4 — Goes cold
"Not much room right now."
"I just wanted this on your radar. Can we revisit in 2 months?" — Plant seed, leave gracefully.
